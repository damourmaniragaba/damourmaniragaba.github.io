// about me

Engineer. Founder.
Builder across borders.

I'm Jean — a CS & Economics student at Cornell originally from Kigali, Rwanda. I write code that ships to real users across East Africa and the United States.

My work spans full-stack web and mobile development, geospatial data engineering, AI systems, and product infrastructure. I care about building things that actually solve problems — whether that's a hospital appointment system reducing patient wait times in Rwanda, or financial inclusion software reaching millions across 6 countries.

Before Cornell, I founded Computer Skills Clinics in Kigali — teaching 100+ students Python, robotics, and ICT. I'm driven by the idea that great software built with intention can level the playing field anywhere in the world.
